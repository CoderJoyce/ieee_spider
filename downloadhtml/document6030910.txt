<div class="section" id="sec3"><div class="header article-hdr"><div class="kicker">		                        SECTION III.</div><h2>Antenna Characteristics</h2></div><p>To confirm the optimized result, we measure the antenna performances such as bandwidth, radiation pattern, and near-field distribution. The measured bandwidth (<inline-formula id=""><tex-math notation="TeX"><span class="MathJax_Preview">{S}_{11} &lt; -</span><span class="MathJax MathJax_Processing" id="MathJax-Element-33-Frame" tabindex="0"></span><script type="math/tex" id="MathJax-Element-33">{S}_{11} &lt; -</script></tex-math></inline-formula>10 dB) is 30 MHz, from 910 to 940 MHz, which entirely covers the required frequency band in Korea (917â923.5 MHz), and it matches well with the simulation result as shown in <a ref-type="fig" anchor="fig3" class="fulltext-link">Fig.Â 3(a)</a>. The operating frequency and input impedance is mainly affected by length of the microstrip line <inline-formula id=""><tex-math notation="TeX"><span class="MathJax_Preview">L_{3}</span><span class="MathJax MathJax_Processing" id="MathJax-Element-34-Frame" tabindex="0"></span><script type="math/tex" id="MathJax-Element-34">L_{3}</script></tex-math></inline-formula>. If <inline-formula id=""><tex-math notation="TeX"><span class="MathJax_Preview">L_{3}</span><span class="MathJax MathJax_Processing" id="MathJax-Element-35-Frame" tabindex="0"></span><script type="math/tex" id="MathJax-Element-35">L_{3}</script></tex-math></inline-formula> increases, then the resonant frequency of the antenna is shifted to lower band, with slight changes of other performances. Thus, the antenna can be tuned for other operating frequency bands, such as Europe (865â868 MHz) and Japan (952â955 MHz). Although this device is designed for near-field applications, the device introduces a certain amount of far-field radiation. Thus, radiation gain patterns are measured to examine the satisfaction of the EIRP regulation. The measured and simulated results are compared in <a ref-type="fig" anchor="fig3" class="fulltext-link">Fig.Â 3(b)</a>, and they agree well with each other. The maximum gain is about 6.8 dBi. Thus, the input power should be lower than 0.832 W (less than 4 W EIRP in Korea). However, the reading performances such as the reading range and reading success tests are conducted with the 1-W input power.</p><div class="figure figure-full" id="fig3"><!--          Workaround for combined images.Eg.- 1000116 Fig. 5        --><div class="img-wrap"><a href="/mediastore/IEEE/content/media/7727/5730210/6030910/6030910-fig-3-large.gif"><img src="/mediastore/IEEE/content/media/7727/5730210/6030910/6030910-fig-3-small.gif" alt="Figure 3" /><div class="zoom" title="View Larger Image"></div></a></div><div class="figcaption"><b class="title">Fig.Â 3. </b><fig><p>(a) Simulated (dashed line) and measured (solid line) reflection coefficient. The measured <inline-formula id=""><tex-math notation="TeX"><span class="MathJax_Preview">{\mbi H}_{z}</span><span class="MathJax MathJax_Processing" id="MathJax-Element-39-Frame" tabindex="0"></span><script type="math/tex" id="MathJax-Element-39">{\mbi H}_{z}</script></tex-math></inline-formula> is greater than the specification of â20 dBA/m in most of the target surface region. As we expected, strong and uniform near <inline-formula id=""><tex-math notation="TeX"><span class="MathJax_Preview">{\mbi H}_{z}</span><span class="MathJax MathJax_Processing" id="MathJax-Element-40-Frame" tabindex="0"></span><script type="math/tex" id="MathJax-Element-40">{\mbi H}_{z}</script></tex-math></inline-formula>-field is observed along the target surface region. We also examine current distribution by EM simulator, and the result shows that the lengths of induced currents on each patch are about half-wavelength and that they flow in the opposite directions, as shown in <a ref-type="fig" anchor="fig4" class="fulltext-link">Fig.Â 4(b)</a>. As the distance <inline-formula id=""><tex-math notation="TeX"><span class="MathJax_Preview">D_{2}</span><span class="MathJax MathJax_Processing" id="MathJax-Element-41-Frame" tabindex="0"></span><script type="math/tex" id="MathJax-Element-41">D_{2}</script></tex-math></inline-formula> between two patches increases, the phase difference between two currents also increases, and thus, <inline-formula id=""><tex-math notation="TeX"><span class="MathJax_Preview">D_{2}</span><span class="MathJax MathJax_Processing" id="MathJax-Element-42-Frame" tabindex="0"></span><script type="math/tex" id="MathJax-Element-42">D_{2}</script></tex-math></inline-formula> should be chosen properly to make currents on two patches with out of phase. Also, the place where the strong <inline-formula id=""><tex-math notation="TeX"><span class="MathJax_Preview">{\mbi H}_{z}</span><span class="MathJax MathJax_Processing" id="MathJax-Element-43-Frame" tabindex="0"></span><script type="math/tex" id="MathJax-Element-43">{\mbi H}_{z}</script></tex-math></inline-formula>-field is generated is easily moved when the length and width of each patch is changed. For example, if we reduce the length <inline-formula id=""><tex-math notation="TeX"><span class="MathJax_Preview">L_{1}</span><span class="MathJax MathJax_Processing" id="MathJax-Element-44-Frame" tabindex="0"></span><script type="math/tex" id="MathJax-Element-44">L_{1}</script></tex-math></inline-formula> or increase the length <inline-formula id=""><tex-math notation="TeX"><span class="MathJax_Preview">L_{2}</span><span class="MathJax MathJax_Processing" id="MathJax-Element-45-Frame" tabindex="0"></span><script type="math/tex" id="MathJax-Element-45">L_{2}</script></tex-math></inline-formula> for raising the induced current of the lower patch, then the strong <inline-formula id=""><tex-math notation="TeX"><span class="MathJax_Preview">H_{z}</span><span class="MathJax MathJax_Processing" id="MathJax-Element-46-Frame" tabindex="0"></span><script type="math/tex" id="MathJax-Element-46">H_{z}</script></tex-math></inline-formula>-field is shifted to the feeder.</p></div>

<div class="section" id="sec5"><div class="header article-hdr"><div class="kicker">		                        SECTION V.</div><h2>Conclusion</h2></div><p>In this letter, we proposed a novel RFID reader antenna for thin-profile shelf. To produce the strong <inline-formula id=""><tex-math notation="TeX"><span class="MathJax_Preview">{\mbi H}_{z}</span><span class="MathJax MathJax_Processing" id="MathJax-Element-48-Frame" tabindex="0"></span><script type="math/tex" id="MathJax-Element-48">{\mbi H}_{z}</script></tex-math></inline-formula>-field over the broad surface of the shelf, the concept of opposite directed currents was employed for the proposed design structure. Also, the coupled microstrip-line feeding system was applied to reduce the antenna profile. The detailed design parameters were optimized to produce the strong <inline-formula id=""><tex-math notation="TeX"><span class="MathJax_Preview">{\mbi H}_{z}</span><span class="MathJax MathJax_Processing" id="MathJax-Element-49-Frame" tabindex="0"></span><script type="math/tex" id="MathJax-Element-49">{\mbi H}_{z}</script></tex-math></inline-formula>-field in the target surface area of 30Ã30 cm<sup>2</sup> using PGA in conjunction with full EM simulator. The optimized antenna showed an average <inline-formula id=""><tex-math notation="TeX"><span class="MathJax_Preview">{\mbi H}_{z}</span><span class="MathJax MathJax_Processing" id="MathJax-Element-50-Frame" tabindex="0"></span><script type="math/tex" id="MathJax-Element-50">{\mbi H}_{z}</script></tex-math></inline-formula>-field of â15 dBA/m over the antenna aperture and achieved stable reading performance. The measured results showed that the proposed antenna is applicable for a thin profile near-field reader antenna.</p></div>
