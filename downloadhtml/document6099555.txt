<div class="section" id="sec1"><div class="header article-hdr"><div class="kicker">		                        SECTION I.</div><h2>Introduction</h2></div><p>For Identification of metallic objects, a radio frequency identification (RFID) tag antenna may have to operate in close proximity to the metallic surface if the tag is placed directly on the surface of a metallic object or on the package of a metallic object. In this scenario, the read range of the RFID tag may decrease seriously because of insufficient energy of electromagnetic wave inspired by the RFID reader antenna <a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref1" id="context_ref_1_1">[1]</a>, <a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref2" id="context_ref_2_1">[2]</a>. Therefore, specific design efforts are required for a UHF RFID tag mountable on metallic surfaces.</p><p>Microstrip patch-type antennas and planar inverted-F (PIF) antennas are the most commonly used antenna structures for UHF RFID tags mountable on metallic surfaces. In <a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref3" id="context_ref_3_1">[3]</a>â<a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref4" id="context_ref_4_1">[4]</a><a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref5" id="context_ref_5_1">[5]</a><a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref6" id="context_ref_6_1">[6]</a><a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref7" id="context_ref_7_1">[7]</a>, UHF RFID tags with regular patches or deformed patches in tag antennas were reported. In <a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref8" id="context_ref_8_1">[8]</a>â<a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref9" id="context_ref_9_1">[9]</a><a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref10" id="context_ref_10_1">[10]</a>, RFID tags with PIF-type antennas taking one-layer structure or two-layer folded radiation body for size reduction were reported. In these designs, a via hole, shorting pin, or plate to ground plane was applied for effective radiation.</p><p>To integrate a UHF tag into the package of metal objects, it is desired that RFID tags can be manufactured directly on package paper with an RFID application-specific integrated circuit (ASIC) embedded, with no shorting pin or via hole used. <div class="section" id="sec5"><div class="header article-hdr"><div class="kicker">		                        SECTION V.</div><h2>Conclusion</h2></div><p>To integrate passive UHF RFID tag into a cigarette pack with aluminum foil in, major design challenge lies in two facts. First, the UHF tag will operate in very close proximity of the metal surface if the package paper is very thin. Second, the size of the UHF tag is strictly restricted so that a large tag is impossible.</p><p>By introducing an additional slot and auxiliary pull-tab strip to facilitate impedance tuning, the requirement of a large metal patch to provide inductance for chip impedance matching can be overcome. A passive UHF RFID tag on 0.5-mm-thick package paper of a cigarette pack with foil in is designed and tested. Due to the increased range for impedance adjustment, the proposed structure can be used to match different RFID chips on packages of different metallic objects.</p></div>
