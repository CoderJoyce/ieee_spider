<div class="section" id="sec1"><div class="header article-hdr"><div class="kicker">		                        SECTION I.</div><h2>Introduction</h2></div><p>This radio frequency identification (RFID) technology has been trendy in order to ease some activities of human beings. It has been applied widely in industrial applications such as the manufacturing industry, security and monitoring systems, and intelligent transportation system (ITS) <a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref1" id="context_ref_1_1">[1]</a>. Typically, an RFID system involves communication between two parties, the reader and transponders or tags. Each of the tags has a unique identity number that is attached to the object or people. The communication process between tags and the reader occurs when they are tuned to the same radio frequency. The RFID reader generates a magnetic field, and when tags pass through the field, the reader will detect that signal and identify the object <a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref2" id="context_ref_2_1">[2]</a>. Therefore, the antenna needs to be carefully designed to ensure good performance of the RFID system. RFID systems can be basically divided into three types according to the tags configuration: active, semi-active, and passive types. They can be operated in different frequency bands in accordance to the standard regulation of an RFID system. Several frequency bands have been assigned for RFID applications, which include 125 kHz, 13.56 MHz, 420â460 MHz, 869 MHz, 902â928 MHz, 2.45 GHz, and 5.8 GHz <a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref3" id="context_ref_3_1">[3]</a>. Recently, UHF and ISM bands are becoming more attractive because of their suitability for long-range application. For that reason, more designs of dual-band antennas for RFID readers were presented to fulfil the requirement of the RFID industry.</p><p>Various methods of archiving the dual-band antenna have been reported <a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref4" id="context_ref_4_1">[4]</a>â<a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref5" id="context_ref_5_1">[5]</a><a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref6" id="context_ref_6_1">[6]</a><a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref7" id="context_ref_7_1">[7]</a><a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref8" id="context_ref_8_1">[8]</a>. Most of the famous methods for producing a dual-band antenna by using the slot configuration on the radiating element have been reported in <a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref4" id="context_ref_4_1">[4]</a> and <a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref5" id="context_ref_5_1">[5]</a>. In <a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref4" id="context_ref_4_1">[4]</a>, an annual plate with a rectangular slot was introduced at the radiating element to make the antenna operate in dual band. The slot has been located at the area in order to reduce current distribution. Therefore, the slot must be carefully designed to ensure successful performance of the antenna.</p><p>In <a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref6" id="context_ref_6_1">[6]</a>â<a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref7" id="context_ref_7_1">[7]</a><a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref8" id="context_ref_8_1">[8]</a>, the approach of aperture coupling was applied to the antenna design to perform as a dual-band antenna. By using this method, the antenna's gain and bandwidth can be improved. However the method requires two substrate boards in order to assemble the aperture-coupled antenna. In <a ref-type="bibr" anchor="ref9" id="context_ref_9_1">[9]</a>, a new design method was proposed in which a U-shaped copper strip was used with unequal arms and a print rectangular ring to perform dual-band with a high-gain antenna. Even though the antenna design shows good performances, its structure is too complex.</p><p>In this letter, a simple and novel structure antenna, operating in the UHF and ISM bands, is proposed for RFID application. The proposed antenna only uses a single substrate board with an aluminium plate as a ground plane. The radiating element is diamond-shaped with no slots introduced on it. The methodology of the proposed antenna design as well as the discussions on both simulation and measurement results are presented in the subsequent sections.</p></div>

<div class="section" id="sec3"><div class="header article-hdr"><div class="kicker">		                        SECTION III.</div><h2>Results and Discussions</h2></div><p><a ref-type="fig" anchor="fig4" class="fulltext-link">Fig.Â 4</a> illustrates the fabrication of the proposed antenna with the optimum value after simulation process. <div class="section" id="sec4"><div class="header article-hdr"><div class="kicker">		                        SECTION IV.</div><h2>Conclusion</h2></div><p>A new and simple marquise-brilliant-diamond-shaped structure dual-band antenna for RFID application is presented in this letter. The size of the antenna is fairly reasonable for both handheld and fixed RFID systems. Dual-band frequencies can be obtained with the simple configuration of this novel design. The size of the radiating element controls the lower frequency band, while the radius of the dual orthogonal quadrant at the edges of the radiating element controls the higher frequency band. The proposed antenna shows good performances in terms of return loss with a â10-dB impedance bandwidth of 18 MHz (902â920 MHz) in UHF band and 80 MHz (2.42â2.5 GHz) in ISM band. Therefore, the antenna can be used for RFID application since it fulfills the standard requirements. The proposed antenna has been fabricated and tested, and it showed the good arrangement between simulated and measured results.</p></div>
